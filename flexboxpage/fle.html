3 Pieces of Advice for Starting and Maintaining Your Own Game Collection.

Home

Keep an Inventory

It's important to keep a running inventory of what you actually have. There are many sites that can help you keep track. I personally use the site Backloggery. The only drawback to it being that you have to enter every game manually. There are plenty of alternative services out there though, so find the one that works best for you.

Proper Storage is Very Important

Older games are fragile. NES games have pins that die very quickly, CDs can be rendered unusable if they're worn down too much, and plastic can age if left in poor conditions. Cartridges should always be in protectors, CDs should always be kept in Jewel cases, and consoles should always be stored in a dry, dark place.

Always Check Before You Buy.

Games are, unfortunately, frequently faked. Spotting fakes is easy, if you know what to look for. When buying cartridge games, especially Nintendo games, always bring either an example of a genuine cartridge, and/or a tool set to open up the game before you buy. Fakes typically do not work properly. CD games should always be checked as well, as CDS are prone to scratching.

That was 3 simple tips for starting and maintaining a game collection.
